Summary

In this scenario, the greatest after-tax value is that of the Miami Heat with a value of $74,273,412, which is $1,366,451 greater than that of Utah, $4,267,023 more than Boston, and $10,439,078 more than Los Angeles. Therefore, Boston ($7,941,425), Utah ($7,998,168), and Los Angeles ($21,306,832) would all need to pay a premium to match the same net value of Miami’s $74,273,412. Miami would be able to offer a discount of $127,892,979 to match Utah’s net value of $72,906,961. Boston would also be able to offer a discount at $135,688,300 over four years.

Five Year Options
To match the same net value of Utah’s fifth year at $39,996,000:

  • A player in Boston would need to receive a fifth year at $45,377,888.
  • A player in Los Angeles would need a salary of $62,362,977.
  • A player in Miami would need a salary of $35,285,731.
